Investigate a Dataset: Survival on the Titanic¶

Kaggle provides an excellent overview on the sinking of the RMS Titanic:

The sinking of the RMS Titanic is one of the most infamous shipwrecks in history. On April 15, 1912, during her maiden voyage, the Titanic sank after colliding with an iceberg, killing 1502 out of 2224 passengers and crew. This sensational tragedy shocked the international community and led to better safety regulations for ships.

One of the reasons that the shipwreck led to such loss of life was that there were not enough lifeboats for the passengers and crew. Although there was some element of luck involved in surviving the sinking, some groups of people were more likely to survive than others, such as women, children, and the upper-class.

Questions for Analysis¶

The current investigation seeks to identify key factors that are associated with the survival of the Titanic passengers. Specifically, the current investigation aims to explore the following questions:

  1. What is the relationship between passengers' sex and their survival?
  2. What is the relationship between passengers' age and their survival?
  3. What is the relationship between passengers' socio-economic status and their survival?
  4. What are the interactions among passengers' sex, age, and socio-economic status, with regard to the passengers' survival?

def categorize_age(age):
    """given a specific age, return a corresponding age group"""

    if age >= 60:
        return "senior"
    elif age >= 18:
        return "adult"
    else:
        return "minor"

# create a new column Age_group
# that documents the age group that each passenger belonged to
df["Age_group"] = df["Age"].apply(categorize_age)

Conclusion¶

The current investigation aims to explore key factors that are associated with the survival of the Titanic passengers. Toward this objective, the current investigation reveals that:

  1. Passengers in the sample that survived seems to be mostly female.
  2. Passengers in the sample that survived seems to be, in many cases, younger than the passengers that did not survive.
  3. Passengers in the sample who survived seems to mostly belong to the upper and middle social classes.
  4. Passengers seems to have a relatively better chance of survival if they were female, if they were under 18 years of age, and if they belonged to the upper and middle social classes.

The current investigation nonetheless suffers from a number of limitations, specifically:

  1. The current investigation is unable to determine how each passenger from the sample dataset was chosen; the current sample might be subject to selection bias.
  2. The current investigation suffers from a considerably reduced sample size, for having to exclude up to 177 passengers with unknown age information from data analysis.
  3. The size of each age group differ drastically in the current sample; for instance, there were 575 adults and only 26 senior passengers, making it almost unsensible to directly compare the survival rates of each age group against each other.
  4. The current iteration of the investigation relies almost exclusively on descriptive analysis and visual exploration; the conclusions drawn from this investigation are not yet supported by rigorous statistical procedures.

Follow-up analysis can examine the relationships and interactions among key variables, using rigorous statistical procedures such as analysis of variance (ANOVA) and multivariate analysis of variance (MANOVA), as well as submitting the dataset through machine learning algorithms.
